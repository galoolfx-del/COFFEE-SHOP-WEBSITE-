About

The story so far

Ember & Oak started as a single drip brewer on a market stall, run on the belief that a neighborhood didn't need another chain — it needed one place that treated coffee like a craft rather than a commodity.

We buy direct from three family farms, roast in small batches twice a week, and train every barista to the same standard before they work the bar alone. That's shown up in a house espresso blend, a rotating seasonal drink, and an 18-hour cold brew — each dialed in by hand, not left on a house setting.

We care about precision over volume — a shot recalibrated for the day's humidity, a pour-over timed to the second, a space that feels considered rather than franchised.

Year One

A market stall and one drip brewer

Started with a single origin, a secondhand grinder, and a folding table three mornings a week.

Year Two

First roastery lease

Moved roasting in-house with a 12kg drum roaster, ending our dependence on outside suppliers.

Year Three

Opened the Foundry Lane café

A permanent counter, pour-over bar, and community table — built for people to actually stay.

Year Five

Direct-trade partnerships formalized

Long-term agreements with three family farms in Yirgacheffe, Huila and Marcala.

Now

Expanding the seasonal program

Testing new seasonal drinks in-house for a month before any of them reach the printed menu.

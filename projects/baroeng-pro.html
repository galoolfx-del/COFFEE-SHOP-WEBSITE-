Ember Espresso Blend

A Yirgacheffe and Huila blend, roasted for a bright, structured shot with a long, syrupy finish — our house standard since year one.

The Problem

Most house espresso blends are built to taste the same every time — which usually means a dark roast that hides the beans rather than showcasing them, and a shot that's the same regardless of what the beans actually need that day.

The Approach

Ember Espresso Blend pairs a washed Yirgacheffe for brightness with a Huila for body, roasted just past first crack to keep the fruit notes intact without the harshness of a lighter roast in milk drinks. Extraction is dialed in every morning, not left on a fixed recipe.

The blend ratio shifts slightly by season as the two origins' harvests come in, so what's on bar in March isn't chemically identical to October — but the tasting profile stays consistent.

Tasting Notes

  • Bright citrus and stone fruit up front from the Yirgacheffe
  • Brown sugar and cocoa body from the Huila underneath
  • Long, syrupy finish that holds up well in milk
  • Consistent, dialed-in extraction rather than a fixed recipe

Sourcing

Both origins come through direct-trade relationships with the same two farms every harvest — no spot-market swaps when prices move.

Availability

On bar every day, as espresso, americano, or the base for any milk drink on the menu.

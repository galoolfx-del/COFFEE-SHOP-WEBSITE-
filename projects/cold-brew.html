Golden Hour Cold Brew

An 18-hour steep of our house blend, finished with a splash of oat cream at service — smooth, low-acid, and never bitter.

The Problem

Most cold brew is just iced hot coffee, or a rushed 6-hour steep that comes out thin and sharp — cold brew done properly needs time, and most cafés don't give it enough.

The Approach

Golden Hour steeps our house blend at room temperature for a full 18 hours in a coarse-mesh immersion brewer, which pulls sweetness and body out of the beans while leaving most of the acidity behind. The concentrate is diluted 1:1 and served over ice with a light pour of oat cream.

Batches are started every evening at close so a fresh one is always ready by the next morning's cold bar rush.

Tasting Notes

  • Naturally sweet, low-acid profile from the long steep
  • Chocolate and dark caramel notes from the house blend
  • Oat cream finish for body without dairy
  • Never over-diluted — full-strength concentrate, not watered down

Process

18-hour room-temperature immersion steep, coarse-mesh filtration, 1:1 dilution with filtered water, served over ice.

Availability

On the cold bar year-round, brewed fresh nightly — ask for it black if you'd rather skip the oat cream.
